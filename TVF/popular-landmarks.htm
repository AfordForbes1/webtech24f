Top Landmarks to Explore in Belize

Belize is home to some of the world's most stunning natural and historical landmarks. Discover the Belize Barrier Reef, the Great Blue Hole, and ancient Mayan ruins such as Altun Ha and Caracol. Experience the breathtaking beauty of these iconic locations, which reflect the rich culture and history of Belize.

Altun Ha

Just 30 miles from Belize City, Altun Ha is one of Belize’s most famous Mayan sites and a symbol of its rich ancient history. The site’s most famous artifact, a jade head depicting the Mayan Sun God, was discovered here and is the largest carved jade object in the Mayan world. Visitors can climb the Temple of the Masonry Altars for a stunning view of the ancient ceremonial grounds and lush surroundings.

The Great Blue Hole

One of Belize’s most iconic natural wonders, the Great Blue Hole is a massive underwater sinkhole located near the center of Lighthouse Reef. Its deep blue waters and mysterious depths make it a bucket-list destination for divers worldwide. Over 400 feet deep and nearly 1,000 feet across, the Great Blue Hole reveals striking limestone formations, stalactites, and a vibrant marine ecosystem.

Caracol

Hidden deep within the Chiquibul Forest Reserve, Caracol is Belize’s largest Mayan archaeological site and one of its most significant. The site includes the towering Caana (“Sky Palace”), which remains the tallest man-made structure in Belize at over 140 feet. Caracol once rivaled the power of the mighty Mayan city Tikal, and its ruins are adorned with intricate carvings and hieroglyphs that tell tales of its rich past.

Xunantunich

Set on a hilltop overlooking the Mopan River, Xunantunich is an ancient Mayan archaeological site that transports visitors back in time. Known for its towering pyramid, El Castillo, which rises over 130 feet, Xunantunich offers panoramic views of Belize’s lush landscape and Guatemala’s distant mountains.

Caye Caulker

Just a short boat ride from Belize City, Caye Caulker is a small island paradise that perfectly encapsulates the laid-back Caribbean lifestyle. Known for its sandy streets, vibrant murals, and easygoing vibe, Caye Caulker invites visitors to "Go Slow" and embrace the island's relaxed pace.

Placencia Peninsula

Known for its stunning beaches, Placencia is a charming village and peninsula that draws travelers seeking relaxation and adventure by the sea. With soft sandy shores, vibrant coral reefs, and a friendly local vibe, Placencia is a perfect base for diving, fishing, and wildlife excursions.

The Sleeping Giant

The Sleeping Giant is a beloved landmark nestled in the Maya Mountains, so named because of its distinct formation resembling a giant lying on its back. Visible from the scenic Hummingbird Highway, this natural wonder captivates travelers with its mythic appearance and majestic presence.
